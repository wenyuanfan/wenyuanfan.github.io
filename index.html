About Me

  • Curriculum Vitae

I am broadly interested in both observational and theoretical seismology. One of my current research focuses is earthquake seismology. Earthquakes are among the most destructive hazards on Earth that occur at both plate boundaries and continent interior with over one quadrillion times energy differences. A better understanding of earthquake processes is one of the most fundamental goals of seismology.

I obtained my Ph.D. from Institute of Geophysics and Planetary Physics, Scripps Institution of Oceanography, UC San Diego, working with Peter Shearer. I am currently a Weston Howland Jr. Postdoctoral Scholar at Woods Hole Oceanographic Institution, working with Jeff McGuire. I also collaborate with Dan Bassett, Catherine de Groot-hedlin, Marine Denolle, Peter Gerstoft, Michael Hedlin, Chen Ji, Junle Jiang, Guy Masters, Diego Melgar, Nathan Miller, Nori Nakata, and Uri ten Brink.
